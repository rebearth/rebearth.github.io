Great Barrier Reef

Coral reef in Queensland, Australia

The Great Barrier Reef, off the coast of Queensland in northeastern Australia, is the largest living thing on Earth, and even visible from outer space. The 2,300km-long ecosystem comprises thousands of reefs and hundreds of islands made of over 600 types of hard and soft coral. It's home to countless species of colourful fish, molluscs and starfish, plus turtles, dolphins and sharks. Unfortunately, we are on the verge of losing this natural wonder due to the lack of positive human intervention.

Taj Mahal

Mausoleum in Agra, India

Flowing next to Taj Mahal (one of the seven wonders of the World), river Yamuna threatens the very existence of the white marble mausoleum in the present times. Despite nationwide discontent, the condition is continuously worsening. With the main causes being agricultural run-off, discharge of industrial effluents and domestic sewage, Yamuna has made not just the Taj loose its glamour, but also made the surroundings inhabitable.

Baoding

City in Heibei, China

Baoding, a city once famous for its Ancient Lotus Lake Garden built in the early years of the Yuan Dynasty (1277 A.D.), is now being awarded the unwanted title of having the worst air quality in whole of china. Housing more than 11 million humans among other life forms, Baoding inhabitants welcomes each day with pollution levels as high as 300 on the air quality index, which is unanimously considered hazardous for human health.

Real Threat?

Are they really some extraterrestrials?

Through this website we urge everyone to share both the constructive and destructive human activities happening around us. Be it beautification of the earth through forest restoration or degrading it through increased global warming. Let's get to know who is doing good and who is screwing it up. Using a smart crowdsourced algorithm, we aim to graphically highlight the places in need of urgent attention.

Just 3 simple steps
1. Click an image around your locality
2. Geo-tag its location
3. Add a line about what is wrong about it
